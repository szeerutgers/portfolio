Typography

The Art of Arranging Type

What is Typography?

Typography is the art and technique of arranging text to make written language readable, clear, and visually appealing. It involves selecting typefaces, adjusting spacing, and organizing content to enhance communication.

Good typography helps guide the reader’s eye, creates structure, and improves the overall design experience.

Key Typography Terms

Typeface
A family of fonts that share a common design.
Font
A specific style and size within a typeface.
Serif
Small decorative strokes at the ends of letters.
Sans-serif
Fonts without decorative strokes.
Kerning
The spacing between individual letters.
Leading
The vertical space between lines of text.
Tracking
The overall spacing between characters.
Hierarchy
The arrangement of text to show importance.
Alignment
The positioning of text on a page.
Baseline
The invisible line that text sits on.
X-height
The height of lowercase letters.
Ascender
Part of a letter that rises above the x-height.
Descender
Part of a letter that falls below the baseline.
